Odili Donald Odita, "Time and Space" (2022)

"Time and Space" is a recent work of art by Nigerian-born, American-based artist Odili Donald Odita. The piece was created in 2022 and is an example of Odita's signature style, which combines bold colors, geometric forms, and abstract compositions.

From a scientific standpoint, the aesthetic appeal of "Time and Space" can be attributed to a number of factors. First, the piece makes use of color theory in a powerful and dynamic way. Odita employs a vibrant palette of bright, contrasting colors such as red, blue, and yellow, which interact with each other to create a sense of energy and movement. This use of color is consistent with the principles of color psychology, which suggest that certain colors can evoke specific emotions and moods.

In addition to its use of color, "Time and Space" is also aesthetically pleasing from a scientific standpoint due to its emphasis on geometric forms and patterns. The composition of the piece is built around a series of rectangles and squares, which are carefully arranged to create a sense of balance and harmony. This emphasis on symmetry and order is consistent with the principles of Gestalt psychology, which suggest that the human brain naturally seeks out patterns and order in visual stimuli.

In his essay "Abstract Art Did Not Begin With Paul Cézanne," John Yau notes that Odita's work is deeply rooted in the history of abstraction, drawing inspiration from a wide range of sources including Islamic art, African textiles, and modernist painting. This fusion of influences is evident in "Time and Space," which combines the geometric abstraction of artists like Kazimir Malevich and Piet Mondrian with the vibrant colors and patterns of traditional African textiles.

Overall, "Time and Space" is a significant work of art that exemplifies Odita's innovative approach to abstraction and his commitment to exploring the intersections between art, culture, and history. From a scientific standpoint, the piece is aesthetically pleasing due to its use of color theory, geometric forms, and principles of visual perception, which combine to create a sense of harmony and balance that is both visually and emotionally satisfying.
